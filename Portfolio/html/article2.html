Introduction

The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt is a RPG single-player game released in 2015 developed by CD Projekt Red, a department of the Polish studio CD Projekt. It is a sequel to the 2011 game The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings inspired by the books of the same title written by Andrzej Sapkowski. The game won the award for Game of the Year 2015 and two expansions were also released to critical acclaim: Hearts of Stone and Blood and Wine. This is one of my top games of all time and because of it I started to read The Witcher books.

Gameplay

The Witcher 3 is a 3rd person role-playing game. You control Geralt of Rivia, a mutant monster-slayer by profession set to defend the people of the Continent, a fictional fantasy world based on the Norse and Slavic mythology populated by humans, dwarves, elves and monsters. Geralt is a master swordsman and uses other weapons such as a crossbow, bombs and two swords. The game is story-driven and has a progression system in the form of a skill tree. Geralt also uses signs, a form of magic that help him overcome even the biggest monsters. The game also has a dialogue tree system meaning players can choose how to interact with non-playable characters and in that way affect the game's ending.

Plot

Continuing the story from the 2nd installment of the game, Geralt and his mentor Vesemir find themselves searching for Ciri, Geralt's adopted daughter. Before going after Ciri they are in search of Yenifer, Geralt's love interest. Ciri is a Child of the Elder Blood, the last heir to an ancient Elven bloodline that grants her the power to manipulate time and space, and is being relentlessly stalked by the enigmatic Wild Hunt.

My Score

I think this game is amazing from start to finish. The gameplay is in balance of being safe and innovative and the combat is fluid. The RPG aspects of the game are well balanced and not too easy but not difficult either. The story is amazing and follows the same overarching story as the first two games in the franchise. However, the game is too long for a casual gamer taking 51 hours to complete and a whopping 173 hours to 100%.
