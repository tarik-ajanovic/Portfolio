Introduction

If you spend any time on the Internet at all you've probably heard recently about artificial intelligence and how it is the frontier of human development and technology. A couple of months back I watched a video about A.I.-generated art and how quickly it became popular and really good, so good in fact that some of the generated images look like an art piece made by humans. Intrigued, I decided to do a bit of research and that's when I found DALLE-2.

What is DALLE-2

DALL-E is an AI system developed by OpenAI that can create original, realistic images and art from a short text description. It can make realistic and context-aware edits, including inserting, removing, or retouching specific sections of an image from a natural language description. It can also take an image and make novel and creative variations of it inspired by the original. When DALLE-2, the improved version of DALLE, first came to be it was not open for public and if one wanted to access it, they needed to apply to enter the waitlist. I of course filled in the form and a couple months later I got the e-mail that I was accepted as a beta tester of the system. After playing around with Dalle for a couple of months I can say that I am blown-away by it.

Each of these pictures was generated by Dalle using the prompts that I put into the system. And yes, the building on the 1st picture really is the Sarajevo City Hall. The text prompts were as follows:

  • Image 1: “Sarajevo city hall at night in cyberpunk style, digital art”
  • Image 2: “a fortune-telling maltese dog reading your fate in a giant hamburger, digital art”
  • Image 3: “A Newcastle United player celebrating scoring a goal in front of the full Saint James' Park stadium”
If you like what you saw you can visit the DALLE-2 gallery and see more AI-generated pictures! As you can see, Dalle even accepts suggestions as to which art style the generated photo should be. But, how does DALLE-2 work?

How does DALLE-2 work

DALL·E was trained by learning the relationship between images and the text used to describe them. It uses a process called diffusion, which starts with a pattern of random dots and gradually alters that pattern towards a final output. Who owns the art and who can use it

The answer is really simple thanks to the cool people at OpenAi. Subject to the Content Policy and Terms, you own the images you create with DALLE, including the right to reprint, sell, and merchandise – regardless of whether an image was generated through a free or paid credit. So if you want to create some cool art head over to www.labs.openai.com and start making some art!
